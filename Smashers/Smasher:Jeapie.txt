   Jeapie (pronounced "Yaw Pee") is a Dutch [23]Melee [24]Captain Falcon player from Groningen, The Netherlands, who is considered to be the best Falcon main in Europe. He is currently ranked 18th on the [25]European Melee Power Rankings and was formerly ranked 3rd on the [26]Dutch Melee Power Rankings, and 66th on the [27]2015 SSBMRank.

Playing style[[36]edit]

   Jeapie is known for his technical and flashy way of playing and is credited as one of the first European players to heavily incorporate [37]shield platform dropping in his game. He is also known for his prowess against [38]Sheik; although Sheik is traditionally considered a difficult matchup for Captain Falcon, he frequently manages to overcome high-level Sheik players. This was particularly made apparent at [39]Republic of Fighters 3, where he made top 8 in a top 32 bracket that pitted him exclusively against Sheik players. At this point he boasts wins over all notable Sheik mains in Europe, including [40]Android, [41]Amsah, and [42]Overtriforce.

Biography[[43]edit]

   Jeapie first started playing Super Smash Bros. Melee upon the game’s PAL release in 2002. In 2006 he started playing competitively, but it wasn’t until 2008 that he entered his first tournament: at [44]X-TC 4 in Almere, he ended up placing 13th. He gradually established himself as one of the better Dutch players, doing well at several locals and placing 13th at his first international tournament, [45]Berlin Calling (April 2010), where a set against fellow Falcon main [46]Pamaro generated a lot of hype ^[47][1]. He would go on to place 9th at [48]ZEST5 and [49]B.E.A.S.T (Sweden) and 7th at [50]OSTS: Saber, before hitting his stride during 2012’s [51]Haags Kwartiertje tournament series, losing grand finals at [52]HK1 to [53]Zgetto and winning [54]HK2 over [55]Remen. He did however reach a low point at [56]HK5 in May 2012, where he got 7th place after a surprising loss to [57]Enig. After this tournament, he decided to take a break from the game, citing diminished enjoyment.

   Jeapie fortunately got his passion for the game back and attended [58]hf.lan 5 in Paris (June 2013), coming back strong at a 5th place. He went on to never place outside top 8 while attending major events such as [59]B.E.A.S.T 4, [60]Republic of Fighters 3 and [61]Avalon I through IV. In June 2014, he was picked up for a sponsorship by LowLandLions ^[62][2].
