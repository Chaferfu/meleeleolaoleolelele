   Super Smash Con 2017 was the 3rd edition of "Super Smash Con", a video game convention and national tournament focused on the [46]Super Smash Bros. series. It was held in Chantilly, Virginia, from August 10th-13th, 2017.

Smash 64 combo contest[[1276]edit]

   Inspired by the NBA Dunk Contest, this unique event allows Smash 64 players to show off their technical skills in front of a large audience.

   Players have 2 minutes to perform any 'combo' of their choosing (a combo is a string of hits that ends in a KO, where the opponent does not touch the ground). Nothing is off limits; all characters, maps and items can be used.

   Once the combo is finished, a panel of 5 judges give them a score from 1-10. The players with the most points move forward in the bracket, while those with the bottom scores are eliminated. There are just 3 rounds of elimination, and the contest usually runs about 1 hour.

   Super Smash Con was the first to introduce the Combo Contest, and it has quickly become a fan-favorite with the videos garnering over 1 million views on [1277]YouTube.

Trivia[[1356]edit]

     * With [1357]Nairo winning both Brawl and Smash 4, this is the first time in the Super Smash Con series that any player has won two separate events at the same tournament.
          + Nairo was also the winner of [1358]Super Smash Con 2016 in Wii U singles.
          + In both the Brawl and Smash 4 events, Nairo came into Grand Finals from losers and went up against a [1359]Diddy Kong player.
     * This is [1360]SuPeRbOoMfAn's lowest placement in Smash 64 singles on North American soil, and the worst overall on a double-elimination setting.
     * Super Smash Con 2017 is currently [1361]Dabuz's and [1362]NAKAT's lowest placements ever in a Smash for Wii U tournament, at 33rd and 65th place, respectively.
     * Similar to [1363]MomoCon 2017, the grand finals in Smash 4 singles was between two previous Super Smash Con champions ([1364]ZeRo, who won [1365]Super Smash Con 2015 and Nairo, who won [1366]Super Smash Con 2016).
     * Both Nairo and [1367]Mango retained their titles from [1368]Super Smash Con 2016 in Wii U singles and Melee singles respectively.
          + However, [1369]Vinnie, winner of Brawl singles in [1370]Super Smash Con 2016, failed to defend his title, finishing at 7th place.
     * Super Smash Con 2017 marks the last national tournament Mango has won as of 2018.
