   The Big House 4 was a [30]Super Smash Bros. Melee and [31]Project M [32]national tournament held in Romulus, Michigan, on October 4th-5th, 2014, hosted primarily by [33]Juggleguy and [34]Strong Bad. In addition to featuring professional smashers from the Midwest region such as [35]Duck, [36]Anther, [37]Kels, [38]VaNz, and [39]Darkrain, the tournament also featured powerful out-of-region talent such as [40]Mango, [41]Mew2King, [42]Hungrybox, [43]Axe, [44]Hax, [45]Westballz, [46]KirbyKaze, [47]Plup, [48]Colbol, [49]SFAT, [50]Lucky, [51]Shroomed, [52]MacD, [53]Zhu, [54]Bladewise, [55]The Moon, [56]Darrell, [57]HugS, and [58]Wizzrobe, as well as international talent such as [59]Armada, [60]Leffen, [61]ZeRo, and [62]Professor Pro.

   The Big House 4 currently stands as [63]one of the largest Melee and Project M tournaments to date, with 574 entrants for Melee and 333 entrants for Project M; additionally, the event was the largest Midwest Melee tournament at the time, surpassing the previous record set by [64]MELEE-FC Diamond in 2007, and the tournament was also the largest to run round-robin pools.

   Amid growing concerns over average gameplay time in version 3.02, The Big House 4 was the first major tournament to use three stocks in Project M games instead of the previous standard of four.

   In the Melee singles bracket, after losing to [65]Mew2King's [66]Sheik 0-3 in winners' semifinals, [67]Mango swept through losers' bracket, defeating [68]Lucky 3-2 in losers' quarterfinals, [69]Armada 3-1 in losers' semifinals, and [70]Leffen 3-1 in losers' finals, finally beating [71]Mew2King in two consecutive 3-1 sets of grand finals to win the tournament. [72]Leffen reached winners' finals for the first time in a major American tournament, defeating top professional players such as [73]Hungrybox and [74]Armada; his performance has solidified his position among the best players of Melee. Hungrybox lost to Leffen 2-3 in winners' quarters, as well as to Lucky 1-3 in losers' bracket, unexpectedly finishing at 9th place, his worst tournament placing since 2007. A large amount of other upsets occurred in Melee singles, with [75]Kels eliminating [76]Axe 3-1 in losers' bracket to make it to top 8, and [77]Kalamazhu making a long run through losers' bracket to secure 9th place, defeating
   [78]KirbyKaze and [79]MacD, before losing in an extremely close set 2-3 against [80]Hax.

Schedule[[100]edit]

   Friday, October 3rd

   9:00 PM: Venue opens
   10:00 PM: Melee It On Me podcast
   12:00 AM: Venue closes

   Saturday, October 4th

   9:00 AM: Venue opens
   11:00 AM: Melee singles pools, Melee doubles pools, Project M singles pools
   3:00 PM: Project M doubles pools
   8:00 PM: Melee singles pools (2nd round), Project M singles pools (2nd round)

   9:00 AM: Venue opens

   1:00 PM: Melee singles bracket top 32, Project M doubles bracket top 8

   7:00 PM: Melee singles bracket top 8
   10:00 PM: Tournament ends

Melee Ruleset[[101]edit]

     * 4 stock, 8:00 minute timer.
     * Items off.
     * Ties after the time limit are determined by stocks, then by percentage.
     * [102]Master Hand is banned.
     * [103]Wobbling is banned.
     * [104]Stalling with [105]Jigglypuff's [106]Rising Pound or [107]Peach's [108]Peach Bomber is banned.
     * Glitches such as [109]Ice Climbers' [110]freeze glitch and [111]Mewtwo's [112]Soul Breaker are banned.
     * [113]Dave's Stupid Rule is in effect.
     * Life stealing is allowed in doubles.
     * [114]Friendly Fire is turned on in doubles.
     * [115]Pause is turned off.

   Stage striking for neutral stages for the first match of the set is done in a "1221" fashion:
    1. First player strikes one stage.
    2. Second player strikes two stages.
    3. First player strikes one stage.

Stage procedure[[121]edit]

   After each match:
    1. Winner announces stage ban (except in best-of-5 sets)
    2. Loser chooses counterpick stage
    3. Winner chooses their character
    4. Loser chooses their character

Project M Ruleset[[122]edit]

     * 3 stock, 8:00 minute timer.
     * All items set to off and item frequency set to NONE.
     * Input Assist set to off.
     * Ties after the time limit are determined by stocks, then by percentage.
     * Dave's Stupid Rule is in effect.
     * Life stealing is allowed in doubles.
     * Friendly Fire is turned on in doubles.
     * Pause is turned off.

   Stage Strike from Starters for the first match of the set in "1221" fashion:
    1. First player strikes one stage.
    2. Second player strikes two stages.
    3. First player strikes one stage
