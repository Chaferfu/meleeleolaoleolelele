<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Methods">Methods</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/index.php?title=2017_SSBMRank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Methods">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Roughly 60 people, such as top players, community leaders, tournament seeders, and regional power rankers, were chosen to be panelists for this edition of <a href="/SSBMRank" title="SSBMRank">SSBMRank</a>. The panelists were asked to rate players based on the following criterion:<br /><i>Based on quality and quantity of results in 2017, rate each player on a scale of 1-10. Imagine if everyone entered a tournament, one hundred times. Who would perform the best?</i><br /> The tournaments were from <a href="/Eden" class="mw-redirect" title="Eden">Eden</a> to (only top 30 could be edited at this point) <a href="/The_Holiday_Bash_Smash_Invitational" class="mw-redirect" title="The Holiday Bash Smash Invitational">The Holiday Bash Smash Invitational</a>. They voted on a 1-10 scale with the best player receiving a "10" and the worst player receiving a "1". Ballots that were not scaled properly were rescaled using an algorithm. The three lowest and three highest ratings before taking the average were removed in order to reduce the variance.
</p>
