   CEO 2016, officially Community Effort Orlando 2016, was the 7th edition of the [29]CEO tournament series, a fighting game tournament held annually in Orlando, Florida, organized by [30]Jebailey. It took place on June 24th-26th, 2016, at the Wyndham Orlando Resort.^[31][1] On December 29th, 2015, Jebailey announced that both [32]Melee and [33]Smash 4 would be once again featured as main games at CEO 2016.^[34][2] On May 27th, 2016, Nintendo of America announced that they would be partnering with CEO for Super Smash Bros. Melee and for Wii U games.^[35][3]

Rules[[63]edit]

Super Smash Bros. Melee[[64]edit]

     * Items are set to off and none. Pause disabled
     * 4 stocks and 8 minute time limit.
     * 2/3 games; 3/5 for Winner's, Loser's, and Grand Finals
     * [65]Wobbling is allowed. The freeze glitch and infinite stalling are banned however.
     * First stage is decided by banning from the Starter Stages in P1-P2-P2-P1 order.
     * Winning player is not locked into the same character for the next match, but has to pick before loser selects character.
     * In a Best of 3 match, a player can ban 1 stage upon winning a game. In a Best of 5 match however, there are no stage bans.
     * Maximum allowed time between games is 60 seconds. Any player violating this rule will be issued a loss.
     * [66]Dave's Stupid Rule: A player cannot counterpick the last stage he/she won on unless mutually agreed to by both players.

Super Smash Bros. for Wii U[[74]edit]

     * Items are set to off and none. [75]Pause and [76]handicap are disabled. [[[Launch rate]] is 100%
     * 2 stocks and 6 minute time limit.
     * 2/3 games; 3/5 for Top 16
     * Customs and Equipment are turned off.
     * [77]Mii Fighters are legal (1-1-1-1 Default Miis only). [78]Amiibo however are banned.
     * All Ω form of stages are banned.
     * First stage is decided by (Rock-Paper-Scissors) best of 1. Winner chooses, then loser chooses twice, then winner. (P1-P2-P2-P1)
     * Following stages decided by winner banning one stage from all stages and then loser choosing.
     * Winning player is not locked into the same character for the next match, but has to pick before loser selects character.
     * Maximum allowed time between games is 60 seconds. Any player violating this rule will be issued a loss.
     * [79]Dave's Stupid Rule: A player cannot counterpick the last stage he/she won on unless mutually agreed to by both players.
     * You may not play on a stage that is not on the available stages list. This will result in a double disqualification.
     * [80]Sudden Death with a tied percentage/stock count will NOT determine the outcome of a tied game. A playoff match with 1 stock/2 minutes will determine the winner.
     * There will be no loading anything on to the tournament consoles.

Trivia[[700]edit]

     * [701]Prince Ramen became the second person (behind [702]Seagull Joe) to have taken a set off of [703]ZeRo in pools in Smash 4.
     * This is the first tournament in Smash 4 history where ZeRo was eliminated before Top 8 singles, finishing at 9th place (being his third worst placing in Smash 4 history, his worst placing being [704]2GGC: Civil War).
     * This is also the first tournament in Smash 4 history that [705]Trela was eliminated in pools. He placed 385th.
     * [706]Abadango, who placed 4th in Smash 4, also participated in Pokkén Tournament, where he placed 5th.
     * This tournament is notable for many top players' poor placings, such as [707]Ally getting 49th, [708]Pink Fresh, [709]K9sbruce, [710]Vinnie, and [711]RAIN getting 33rd, [712]Marss, and [713]FOW getting 25th, [714]ESAM, [715]Tweek, and [716]Nairo getting 17th, [717]ZeRo getting 9th, and [718]Trela, [719]6WX, [720]Seagull Joe, and [721]MVD drowning in pools.
     * This was [722]Ally's worst placing at a Smash 4 tournament in his career, placing at 49th place.
