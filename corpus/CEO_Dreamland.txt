   CEO Dreamland, officially Community Effort Orlando Dreamland, was a tournament held at the Wyndham Orlando Resort in Orlando, Florida, on April 14th-16th, 2017. It was announced on November 29th, 2016^[47][1], and registration opened on December 16th.

   The event was held just before [48]CEO's 8th main edition, [49]CEO 2017. The event was almost entirely focused on Smash, featuring singles and doubles tournaments for [50]Super Smash Bros, [51]Super Smash Bros. Melee, and [52]Super Smash Bros. for Wii U as well as side events for Rivals of Aether and Brawlhalla. On the announcement day, Jebailey stated that he would add [53]Super Smash Bros. Brawl to the game lineup if his tweet reached 5000 retweets^[54][2]. It eventually did on November 30th and Brawl singles and doubles were added as an official event^[55][3].

Aftermath[[71]edit]

   [72]CEOGaming reported a $20,000 loss following the CEO Dreamland event a few days after it ended. CEOGaming owner [73]Alex Jebailey stated that Dreamland would need a minimum of 2,000 attendees in order to be considered a success. He also stated that there are no plans to run a follow-up event any time soon. However, Jebailey did mention that Dreamland scheduling did conflict with Easter Holiday and [74]Star Wars Celebration which resulted in it being the first technical failing for a CEOGaming event. This failing is also due to the significant amount of quality put into the event.^[75][4]^[76][5]
