   Evolution 2015, or simply EVO 2015, was the 14th edition of the [24]Evolution Championship Series, a fighting game tournament held annually in Las Vegas, Nevada, which took place on July 17th to 19th, 2015 at the [25]Bally's Las Vegas / [26]Paris Las Vegas Casino. It featured [27]Super Smash Bros. Melee and [28]Super Smash Bros. for Wii U singles, as well as a Melee doubles side-event.

   EVO 2015 currently stands as the [29]second largest Melee and Smash 4 tournament to date, with 1,926 entrants for Smash 4, 1,869 entrants for Melee, and 3,284 unique players across both games. Contrary to expectations that there would be a large overlap on the player base for both games, [30]Joey Cuellar, EVO's tournament director, confirmed that only 517 players entered both games, an overlap of only 16%.^[31][1]^[32][2]

   EVO 2015 allowed [33]custom special moves in Super Smash Bros. for Wii U by utilizing the [34]Official Custom Moveset Project, and was notably the last major tournament to use the project. Furthermore, several of the game's [35]DLC characters, [36]Lucas, [37]Roy, and [38]Ryu, were not allowed due to their then-recent release^[39][3].

Official game lineup[[59]edit]

   The official game lineup for EVO 2015 was announced on January 20th, by Joey Cuellar, during a live stream on Twitch.tv, with Mike Ross as host. Notably for this year's edition was the inclusion of two Smash games on the official lineup, for the first time, with the return of [60]Super Smash Bros. Melee alongside [61]Super Smash Bros. for Wii U. The decision to include both games was made after continued interest in Melee was noted by the staff, following the release of Super Smash Bros. for Wii U, with the record breaking numbers for [62]Apex 2015.^[63][4]

Ruleset[[74]edit]

[75]Super Smash Bros. Melee[[76]edit]

     * Items are set to off and none. Pause disabled
     * 4 [77]stocks and 8 minute [78]time limit.
     * 2/3 games; 3/5 for Winner's, Loser's, and Grand Finals
     * [79]Wobbling is allowed. The freeze glitch and infinite stalling are banned however.
     * First stage is decided by banning from the Starter Stages in P1-P2-P2-P1 order.
     * Winning player is not locked into the same character for the next match, but has to pick before loser selects character.
     * In a Best of 3 match, a player can ban 1 stage upon winning a game. In a Best of 5 match however, there are no stage bans.
     * Maximum allowed time between games is 60 seconds. Any player violating this rule will be issued a loss.
     * [80]Dave's Stupid Rule: A player cannot counterpick the last stage he/she won on unless mutually agreed to by both players.

Super Smash Bros. for Wii U[[88]edit]

     * Items are set to off and none. [89]Pause and [90]handicap are disabled. [91]Launch rate is 1.0.
     * 2 stocks and 6 minute time limit.
     * 2/3 games; 3/5 for Winner's, Loser's, and Grand Finals
     * Customs are legal and must follow the [92]Official Custom Moveset Project. Equipment however is turned off.
     * [93]Lucas, [94]Roy, and [95]Ryu are banned. [96]Amiibo are also banned.
     * All Ω form of stages are banned.
     * Order of events:Pick characters, then stage striking, then custom move selection.
     * First stage is decided by (Rock-Paper-Scissors) best of 1. Winner strikes, then loser strikes, then winner, then loser, then winner. (P1-P2-P1-P2-P1)
     * Following stages decided by winner banning two stages from all stages and then loser choosing.
     * Winning player is not locked into the same character for the next match, but has to pick before loser selects character.
     * Maximum allowed time between games is 60 seconds. Any player violating this rule will be issued a loss.
     * [97]Dave's Stupid Rule: A player cannot counterpick the last stage he/she won on unless mutually agreed to by both players.
     * You may not play on a stage that is not on the available stages list. This will result in a double disqualification.
     * Maximum allowed time between games is 60 seconds. Any player violating this rule will be issued a loss.
     * Sudden Death with a tied percentage/stock count will NOT determine the outcome of a tied game. A playoff match with 1 stock/2 minutes will determine the winner.
     * Allowed controllers: [98]Wii U Pro Controller & [99]GameCube Controller.
     * There will be no loading anything on to the tournament consoles.

Trivia[[826]edit]

     * [827]ZeRo, who won the [828]Super Smash Bros. for Wii U event, did not lose a single game in tournament.
     * Nintendo of America marketing manager and translator [829]Bill Trinen registered to participate in the SSB4 tournament, under the tag "Th | trintroll",^[830][5] with the "Th" crew tag apparently standing for [831]Treehouse. However, due to the passing of [832]Satoru Iwata, president of Nintendo, Trinen changed his plans and did not attend, as EVO was held on the same weekend as Iwata's funeral.^[833][6]
          + In response to Satoru Iwata's passing, a moment of silence was held before Smash 4's top 8.
     * MadCatz once again gave out gold FightSticks for EVO winners this year. The top 3 of each official EVO game, including both Smash games, received one.^[834][7]^[835][8]^[836][9]
     * After receiving his 5th place medal in [837]Melee singles, [838]Mango took it off and threw it into the crowd.
     * [839]Plup humorously forgot to appear on stage to receive his medal with the rest of the top 8 players.
     * [840]PC Chris did not attend the tournament, although imposters attended in the Melee and Smash 4 tournaments using his name.
     * This tournament marks highest ever placing of a [841]Pac-Man main at a Smash 4 national, with [842]Abadango placing 4th.
