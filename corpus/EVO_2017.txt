   Evolution 2017, or simply EVO 2017, was the 16th edition of the [21]Evolution Championship Series, a fighting game tournament held annually in Las Vegas, Nevada. The event took place on July 14th to 16th, 2017, at the [22]Mandalay Bay Events Center. It was announced on January 24th, by EVO's tournament director Joey "[23]Mr. Wizard" Cuellar, that both [24]Super Smash Bros. Melee and [25]Super Smash Bros. for Wii U would be featured as official games on the EVO 2017 game lineup. The ninth game was chosen via a donation drive with the proceeds gone to the [26]Make-A-Wish Foundation. The [27]Disney XD channel broadcasted Smash 4 Top 8 live on Sunday evening, which had been confirmed by a tweet from the official Disney account. The Smash 4 Top 8 was also broadcasted live on the [28]ESPNU channel. These are the first two instances of a Smash 4 tournament being televised on a platform other than the Internet.^[29][1]^[30][2]^[31][3]

Ruleset[[57]edit]

Super Smash Bros. Melee[[58]edit]

     * Items are set to off and none. [59]Pause disabled
     * 4 [60]stocks and 8 minute [61]time limit.
     * 2/3 games; 3/5 for Winner's, Loser's, and Grand Finals
     * [62]Wobbling is allowed. The freeze glitch and infinite stalling are banned however.
     * First stage is decided by banning from the Starter Stages in P1-P2-P2-P1 order.
     * Winning player is not locked into the same character for the next match, but has to pick before loser selects character.
     * In a Best of 3 match, a player can ban 1 stage upon winning a game. In a Best of 5 match however, there are no stage bans.
     * Maximum allowed time between games is 60 seconds. Any player violating this rule will be issued a loss.
     * [63]Dave's Stupid Rule: A player cannot counterpick the last stage he/she won on unless mutually agreed to by both players.

Super Smash Bros. for Wii U[[71]edit]

     * Items are set to off and none. [72]Pause and [73]handicap are disabled. [74]Launch rate is 1.0.
     * 2 [75]stocks and 6 minute [76]time limit.
     * 2/3 games; 3/5 for Winner's, Loser's, and Grand Finals
     * Customs and Equipment are turned off.
     * [77]Mii Fighters are legal (1-1-1-1 Default Miis only). [78]Amiibo however are banned.
     * First stage is decided by (Rock-Paper-Scissors) best of 1. Winner strikes, then loser strikes twice, then winner. (P1-P2-P2-P1)
     * Following stages decided by winner banning one stage from all stages and then loser choosing.
     * Winning player is not locked into the same character for the next match, but has to pick before loser selects character.
     * Maximum allowed time between games is 60 seconds. Any player violating this rule will be issued a loss.
     * [79]Dave's Stupid Rule: A player cannot counterpick the last stage he/she won on unless mutually agreed to by both players.
     * You may not play on a stage that is not on the available stages list. This will result in a double disqualification.
     * If a game ends with a [80]self-destruct move, the results screen will determine the winner.
     * [81]Sudden Death with a tied percentage/stock count will NOT determine the outcome of a tied game. A playoff match with 1 stock/2 minutes will determine the winner.
     * There will be no loading anything on to the tournament consoles.

Trivia[[661]edit]

     * [662]Bear, who is the main [663]TO for [664]Super Smash Bros. for Wii U, was invited as a guest during the EVO 2017 lineup announcement to comment about Smash 4 and the fixes the staff at EVO would do in order to improve on the title's tournament performance [665]from the previous year.
     * This marked the first time that the [666]Super Smash Bros. for Wii U finals will be held on Sunday, and the [667]Super Smash Bros. Melee finals will be held on Saturday.
     * This is the fifth consecutive appearance for [668]Super Smash Bros. Melee at EVO (sixth overall), as well being the third consecutive appearance for [669]Super Smash Bros. for Wii U.
     * EVO 2017 is the 10 year anniversary of Melee 's original appearance at [670]EVO 2007.
     * Hungrybox was yellow carded following his set with [671]SFAT due to [672]Crunch appearing on-stage as his coach [673]after the first game when the rules disallowed coaching after round 2 pools. This decision was met with controversy as the rules regarding coaching past pools would result in a match loss for the coached player which Hungrybox did not receive. However, SFAT was also allegedly being coached by [674]G$, which resulted in [675]the match standing; this led to even more controversy, as many people agreed that SFAT was not being coached at all by G$, as [676]the action performed did not qualify under the definition. Crunch also stated that he was unaware of the rules.
          + Furthermore, the [677]official EVO rules state that any incident should be reported at the time it occurs, and will otherwise be ignored, so technically, Hungrybox should not have been carded at all.
     * EVO 2017 stands as one of [678]MkLeo's worst Smash 4 tournaments, placing 65th (the other being [679]2GGC: Civil War where he also placed 65th).
     * EVO 2017 also stands as [680]Ally's worst Smash 4 tournament in 2017, placing 49th since placing the same at [681]CEO 2016 over a year prior.
     * At the beginning of Smash 4's Top 8, [682]Twitch experienced [683]serious server issues, due to the large number of people around the globe who attempted to watch the event.^[684][4]
     * [685]Salem's run through this EVO's Smash 4 top 8 shares many similarities with [686]Hungrybox's Melee performance at EVO the year prior. They both lost their winners semis matches by 0-2 against the #6 ranked player ([687]Larry Lurr and [688]Plup, respectively), then won their losers quarters matches against the top-ranked solo mains of their respective characters by 2-0 ([689]KEN and [690]S2J at the time), won their losers semis matches by 2-1 after losing game 1, won their losers finals matches against the players who sent them to the losers bracket by 3-1 after losing game 1, and seized the tournament victory against the #1 ranked player through two grand finals sets of five games each ([691]ZeRo and [692]Armada, who also both won an EVO before), while also sharing the same game victory order during the second set. Much like Hungrybox's famous comeback against Armada on game 5 of their first set of grand finals at [693]EVO 2016, Salem also pulled an unlikely comeback against ZeRo on
       game 5 to win, albeit on the second set. Additionally, both Salem and Hungrybox are from Florida, and both are known for their slower, patient playstyle.
          + Salem's run also bears similarities to Nairo's finish at [694]MLG World Finals 2015. Both in Grand Finals reset against ZeRo, and both ending the set with an off-the-top blast zone KO from a relatively low percent.
          + This is also the second Smash 4 tournament where Salem has double-eliminated ZeRo, the first being [695]Collision XIV.
     * ZeRo temporarily deactivated his Twitter account shortly after the conclusion of Smash 4's grand finals. Although a number of observers believed he had done so to avoid harassment over his loss, ZeRo reactivated his account later in the day, stating [696]he only deactivated his account to relax and that [697]he did not want to worry anyone.
     * [698]D1, who commentated with [699]TKBreezy for the [700]2015 and [701]2016 [702]Super Smash Bros. for Wii U finals as well as [703]2014's [704]Super Smash Bros. Melee finals (with [705]Prog), ended up commentating on DisneyXD/ESPNU this year alongside [706]VikkiKitty. On [707]Twitch, TK would be joined by his Black Plague partner [708]EE.
          + Furthermore, EVO 2017 is the first tournament for any Super Smash Bros. game to be broadcast on live television ([709]Super Smash Bros. for Wii U finals were broadcast on DisneyXD and ESPNU).
