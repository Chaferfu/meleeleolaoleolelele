   Evolution 2018, or simply EVO 2018, was the 17th edition of the [22]Evolution Championship Series, a fighting game tournament held annually in Las Vegas, Nevada. EVO 2018 is scheduled to take place on August 3rd to 5th, 2018, at the [23]Mandalay Bay Events Center. It was announced on February 6th, by EVO's tournament director Joey "[24]Mr. Wizard" Cuellar, that both [25]Super Smash Bros. Melee and [26]Super Smash Bros. for Wii U would be featured as official games on the EVO 2018 game lineup. The event will see the return of Melee on the Sunday finals, as Smash 4 was on the main stage in the previous year.^[27][1]^[28][2] EVO 2018 featured a playable demo of [29]Super Smash Bros. Ultimate, courtesy of [30]Nintendo, and a Super Smash Bros. Ultimate [31]Nintendo Direct was announced for August 8, 2018 prior to the Super Smash Bros. Melee finals.

Controversy[[743]edit]

   The event came under controversy during the grand finals match of the [744]Super Smash Bros. for Wii U event between [745]Lima and [746]CaptainZack. Neither player seemed to take the match seriously, as evidenced by their lack of staidness throughout the set and by Lima granting CaptainZack a "homie [747]stock" during game 5 of the first set. Additionally, during game 1 of the second set, both players held [748]Bullet Climax in place for nearly two minutes, which caused [749]tournament organizer [750]Bear to [751]come on stage and issue both players a [752]yellow card. Many top players asserted that this set tarnished the competitive integrity of the event,^[753][3]^[754][4] and most of the audience members attending EVO had [755]left the venue before or during grand finals. CaptainZack apologized after the match for his "disrespectful showing", but stated that he was "truly trying his best" throughout the set. ^[756][5] Lima also apologized for giving a "homie stock" to CaptainZack, saying
   that he did it because he was playing one of his best friends and didn't want the match to end with a [757]self-destruct. Moreover, he explained that it wasn't his intention to "make grand finals look like collusion or childish", but that he simply "play[s] better when [he] play[s] to have fun".^[758][6]

Trivia[[759]edit]

     * This is the first year where the lineup announcement stream was not co-hosted by Mike Ross. Instead, Tekken Project and EVO staff member Mark "MarkMan" Julio was the co-host.
     * This is the first EVO since 2014 that doesn't feature a total of nine games in the main EVO lineup.
     * [760]Super Smash Bros. Ultimate was confirmed to replace [761]Super Smash Bros. for Wii U in the event if it was released in time for EVO 2018.^[762][7] However, this did not take effect as it was released four months after EVO 2018.
     * This is the first time [763]Armada has lost to a player outside the [764]Summer 2018 MPGR top 6 or PPMD since [765]Amsah in 2010 (excluding sandbagging), as [766]Swedish Delight defeated Armada in winners top 24.
          + Armada embarked on an impressive losers run after his loss to Swedish Delight, defeating [767]Gahtzu, [768]Mew2King, [769]Zain, [770]S2J, [771]Mango, [772]Hungrybox, and [773]Plup before losing to Leffen 0-3 in grand finals.
     * This is the first time where none of the [774]Five Gods won Melee singles at EVO post-EVO 2013, with [775]Leffen defeating Armada in grand finals.
          + This is also the first EVO to have no American players in Melee grand finals.
          + This is also the first time since [776]CEO Dreamland where [777]Salem placed outside of the top 12, where he got 17th place.
     * There was a poll for which game [778]Arc System Works president Minoru Kidooka should enter. [779]Super Smash Bros. Melee ended up taking 1st place in the poll and Kidooka entered said game.^[780][8] He placed 721st out of 1,353 entrants.^[781][9]
